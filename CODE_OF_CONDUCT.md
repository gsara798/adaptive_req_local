# Code of Conduct

## Our commitment

We are committed to providing a respectful, inclusive, and professionally
constructive environment for everyone participating in this project, regardless
of background, identity, career stage, institution, or level of experience.

## Expected behavior

Participants are expected to:

- communicate respectfully and focus criticism on ideas, methods, and code;
- welcome questions and differences in technical background;
- acknowledge uncertainty, limitations, and appropriate scientific attribution;
- provide feedback that is specific, actionable, and evidence-based;
- respect privacy, intellectual property, research ethics, and data-use
  restrictions;
- avoid presenting research software as clinically validated when it is not.

## Unacceptable behavior

Unacceptable behavior includes:

- harassment, intimidation, discrimination, or personal attacks;
- insulting or dismissive comments directed at individuals;
- unwanted disclosure of private or identifying information;
- deliberate disruption of technical discussions;
- plagiarism or misrepresentation of authorship, results, or validation;
- uploading confidential, clinical, proprietary, or unauthorized data;
- other conduct that would reasonably be considered inappropriate in a
  professional or academic setting.

## Scope

This code applies in repository issues, pull requests, reviews, discussions,
documentation, and other project-related communication.

## Reporting

Concerns may be reported privately to the project maintainer through the contact
information associated with the maintainer's GitHub profile. Reports will be
reviewed in good faith and handled as confidentially as reasonably possible.

## Enforcement

The maintainer may edit or remove contributions that violate this code and may
temporarily or permanently restrict participation when necessary. Enforcement
decisions will consider the severity, context, and recurrence of the behavior.

## Attribution

This project-specific code of conduct is informed by common open-source and
academic community standards.
