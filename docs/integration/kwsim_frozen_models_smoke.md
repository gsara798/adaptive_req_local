# KWSIM to Frozen Adaptive REQ Smoke Integration

**Date:** 2026-07-20

## Objective

Verify that a complex displacement field exported by the
`shear-wave-simulation-framework` can be loaded, processed by the native REQ
implementation, passed through the frozen adaptive quantile models, and
converted into shear-wave-speed estimates.

This is an interface and inference smoke test. It is not a training experiment
or a generalization claim.

## Input

- Physical simulation dimension: 3D
- Exported measurement: central x-z displacement plane
- Public orientation: `[Nz,Nx]`
- Field size: `42 x 36`
- Frequency: `500 Hz`
- Spatial sampling: `dx = dz = 0.5 mm`
- Homogeneous truth: `2.0 m/s`
- REQ window parameter: `M = 2`
- Operational speed guess: `3.0 m/s`
- REQ center spacing: `1.0 mm`
- Number of evaluated windows: `54`

## Frozen Model Bundle

Relative local path:

`outputs/baseline_minimal_v1/models/baseline_minimal_v1_q_models.mat`

The following frozen models were evaluated:

- `q_spectrum_only`
- `q_spectrum_plus_composition`

The model bundle was used without retraining, recalibration, or parameter
tuning.

## Results

### Discrete SingleWave theory

- Mean quantile: `0.67151`
- Mean SWS: `1.91457 m/s`
- MAPE: `4.2716%`
- Bias: `-4.2716%`
- CoV: `0.4067%`
- Maximum APE: `4.8850%`
- Valid windows: `54 of 54`

### q spectrum only

- Mean quantile: `0.62589`
- Mean SWS: `1.97350 m/s`
- MAPE: `1.3248%`
- Bias: `-1.3248%`
- CoV: `0.4233%`
- Maximum APE: `2.0483%`
- Valid windows: `54 of 54`

### q spectrum plus composition

- Mean quantile: `0.62266`
- Mean SWS: `1.97750 m/s`
- MAPE: `1.1249%`
- Bias: `-1.1249%`
- CoV: `0.3178%`
- Maximum APE: `1.6857%`
- Valid windows: `54 of 54`

## Interpretation

The discrete SingleWave quantile underestimated SWS by approximately `4.3%`.

The simulated field was generated by a finite contact in a 3D elastodynamic
domain and is therefore not an ideal mathematical plane wave.

Both frozen adaptive models selected lower quantiles and substantially reduced
the negative bias.

For this case, the composition-augmented model was slightly more accurate and
spatially more stable than the spectrum-only model.

These results establish compatibility between the KWSIM export contract and
the adaptive REQ inference pipeline.

They do not establish model performance across source geometries, frequencies,
heterogeneous media, attenuation, or noise.
